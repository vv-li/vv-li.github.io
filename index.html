Who am I

Most days, I spend my time as an economist conducting research on labour markets and socioeconomic outcomes. Outside of that, the things that occupy my time the most are music, photography, reading, clicking maps, and planning my next adventure (and now, this site, I suppose). I have a fascination with people and places, and to gain perspective across cultures, languages, geography and time is a purpose which I am privileged to pursue. In many ways, I hope my travels help shape me into a better person; to grow more patient and give grace, expand my musical palette, appreciate nature, become a more well-practiced photographer, and a sharper economist when researching people-centered issues. I'd be honoured to share this journey with anyone who'd like to tag along (in spirit, of course).
